Andrea E. (Borda) Stanford

Unknown - August 14, 2021

Biography

Andrea earned a B.S. in Business Administration from Pepperdine in 1988, then worked as an institutional specialty pharmaceutical representative at Pfizer in Los Angeles before entering UCLA Anderson, where she earned her MBA in 1996. Over the next two decades she became a notable Los Angeles consumer-brand and digital-commerce executive, holding senior roles across pharmaceuticals, e-commerce, editorial design, and consumer brands. She served on the Los Angeles board of Camp Kesem, the national nonprofit supporting children of parents with cancer.

Obituary

Andrea E. (Borda) Stanford, of Los Angeles, California, died on August 14, 2021, after a battle with pancreatic cancer. A graduate of Pepperdine University (B.S. 1988) and the UCLA Anderson School of Management (M.B.A. 1996), Andrea was a brand strategist and creative executive whose 20-year career bridged editorial design, e-commerce, and consumer-brand leadership. After early years at Pfizer and at eStyle/Babystyle.com, she held senior roles at the Walt Disney Company, helped launch C Magazine, and went on to serve as VP of Merchandising and General Manager of Hunters Alley at One Kings Lane and as SVP of Marketing & Partnerships at Everything But The House. In 2018 she was named Senior Vice President and General Manager of Society6 by Leaf Group. She was a board member of Camp Kesem-Los Angeles. Andrea is survived by her husband, Steven Stanford, and her three children — Ryan, Brooke, and Jade — and was preceded in death by her father, John W. Borda. A Project Purple memorial fund was created in her honor to advance pancreatic-cancer research. Andrea was known for her warmth, her impeccable taste, and her ability to build teams and brands with equal grace.
