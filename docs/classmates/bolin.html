Gregory P. Bolin

1964 - June 6, 1996

Biography

Greg grew up in Fountain Valley, California, where he was a first-team all-county high school tight end. He earned an athletic scholarship to UCLA, played on Rose Bowl and Fiesta Bowl teams, and graduated in 1987 with a B.A. in English. He went on to earn a secondary teaching credential from CSU Long Beach in 1990, then taught English and served as an assistant football coach at Mater Dei High School in Santa Ana. He returned to UCLA for his MBA at the Anderson School of Management. At the time of his death he was completing the program and had accepted an investment-banking role with Lehman Brothers in San Francisco. He died on June 6, 1996, age 31, killed riding his bicycle. UCLA Anderson awarded him his MBA posthumously.

Obituary

Gregory P. "Greg" Bolin, 31, of Fountain Valley, California, was killed in a bicycle accident on Thursday, June 6, 1996, weeks short of completing his MBA at the UCLA Anderson School of Management. A 1987 graduate of UCLA — where he played on Rose Bowl and Fiesta Bowl football teams after a standout high-school career as a first-team all-county tight end — Greg returned to teaching after college, earning a secondary credential from CSU Long Beach in 1990 and joining Mater Dei High School in Santa Ana as an English teacher and assistant football coach. He had recently accepted an investment-banking position with Lehman Brothers in San Francisco. Greg was an athlete's athlete and a teacher's teacher: loyal, hard-working, and generous with his time. He is buried at Pacific View Memorial Park in Corona del Mar. UCLA Anderson awarded him his MBA posthumously, and in 2006 his classmates and family established the Greg Bolin Fellowship at Anderson in his memory.

Memorial Legacy

In 2006 — ten years after his death — UCLA Anderson established the Greg Bolin Fellowship, supporting MBA students in his memory.
